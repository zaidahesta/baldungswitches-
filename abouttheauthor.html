About the author

My name is Zaida Hesta and I am a Research Master's Student at Utrecht University specializing in late Medieval art.
I completed my Bachelor's in Art History in Utrecht, where I focused on European art of the late Middle Ages and early Renaissance.
My main interests are showcased in my Bachelor's thesis on Hans Baldung Grien's Witches (see 'Sources and Further Reading').
In this thesis I investigated the influence of the religious upheaval in Early Modern Strasbourg on the creation of Hans Baldung's 'witch-works'.
My future research will focus on the crossroads of religious conflict, witchcraft and gender relations in late Medieval and early Renaissance art.

I live in the beautiful city of Utrecht, home to wonderful museums like the Catharijneconvent and the Centraal Museum, and a great concert hall, Tivoli Vredenburg. In my free time I like to read indiscriminately, play DnD, mess around on the internet and play guitar (badly). I also try not to drink too much coffee, but that seems to be a losing battle.
