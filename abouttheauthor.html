About the author

Zaida Hesta completed her Bachelor’s in Art History at Utrecht University, where she focused on European art of the late Middle Ages and early Renaissance. She is currently enrolled in the MRes programme Ancient, Medieval and Renaissance Studies at Utrecht University, specializing in Medieval Art. Her main interests are showcased in her bachelor’s thesis, which investigates the influence the religious upheaval in Early Modern Strasbourg had on the creation of Hans Bandung’s artworks of witches. Zaida Hesta’s future research will focus on the crossroads of religious conflict, witchcraft and gender relations in late Medieval and early Renaissance art.
